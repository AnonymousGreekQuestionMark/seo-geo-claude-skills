# Business Language Glossary

> Maps technical SEO/GEO terminology to client-friendly business language.
> Use these translations in HTML tooltips, PDF introductions, and executive summaries.

## Framework Names

| Technical Term | Business Term | When to Use |
|----------------|---------------|-------------|
| CITE | Domain Authority Score | Always in client reports |
| CORE-EEAT | Content Quality Score | Always in client reports |
| GEO Score | AI Visibility Score | When discussing AI citation readiness |
| SEO Score | Search Visibility Score | When discussing traditional search rankings |

## CITE Dimensions

| Dimension | Technical Name | Business Name | Plain English |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| C | Citation Strength | Reputation & References | How often credible sources link to and mention you |
| I | Identity Clarity | Brand Recognition | How clearly search engines and AI identify your company |
| T | Trust Signals | Credibility Indicators | Security, accuracy, and reliability markers |
| E | Expertise Evidence | Authority Proof | Demonstrated knowledge and industry standing |

## Score Interpretations

### For Client Reports

Instead of: "CITE score: 72/100, verdict: CAUTIOUS"
Write: "Domain Authority: 72/100 (Moderate Authority) — Your site has solid credibility but can improve in key areas."

Instead of: "CORE-EEAT C:75 O:80 R:65 E:70"
Write: "Content Quality: Your content scores well on structure (80) and relevance (75), with opportunities to improve verifiability (65)."

Instead of: "GEO Score: 68/100"
Write: "AI Visibility: 68/100 — AI assistants cite your content moderately well; targeted improvements can increase this significantly."

### Priority Translations

| Priority | Technical Context | Client-Friendly |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| P0 | Veto items, blockers | Critical — address this week |
| P1 | High-impact fixes | Important — plan for this month |
| P2 | Optimization opportunities | Recommended — schedule this quarter |
| P3 | Nice-to-haves | Optional — consider when time allows |

## Usage Guidelines

1. **Executive Summary**: Always use business terms; avoid raw dimension codes
2. **Detailed Sections**: Use business term first, technical code in parentheses for reference
3. **Tooltips**: Business language only; no codes or abbreviations
4. **PDF Appendix**: Technical codes acceptable since this is reference material
5. **Score Displays**: Show numeric score + business verdict, not just numbers

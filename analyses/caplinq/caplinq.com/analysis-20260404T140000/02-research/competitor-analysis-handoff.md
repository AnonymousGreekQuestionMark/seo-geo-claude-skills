### CAPLINQ Competitive Strengths
1. **Deep technical blog content** (290 posts) — rivals most competitors in content depth for niche topics
2. **REACH compliance expertise** — rare differentiator; few distributors offer full EU REACH representation
3. **Multi-supplier curation** — unlike direct manufacturers, CAPLINQ aggregates best-in-class from multiple suppliers
4. **Cold supply chain capability** — unique capability for temperature-sensitive materials
5. **Customer trust signals** — Apple, Intel, Honeywell, NXP as named clients

### CAPLINQ Competitive Weaknesses
1. **Brand authority** — No Wikipedia, thin Knowledge Panel vs. established distributors
2. **Scale** — Smaller catalog than Digi-Key/Grainger; cannot compete on breadth
3. **Content organization** — Blog content is strong but not organized into pillar pages / topic clusters
4. **Backlink profile** — Likely weaker than major competitors; CITE Citation dimension gap
5. **Social presence** — B2B social signals likely weak vs. larger competitors

### Content Gap vs. SpecialChem (primary content competitor)
- SpecialChem has extensive "guide" and "how-to" content for material selection
- CAPLINQ has deep product expertise but lacks structured "definitive guide" pillar pages
- Opportunity: "CAPLINQ Guide to Thermal Interface Materials" as a GEO-optimized pillar page that outranks SpecialChem's generic coverage

### Action Plan
1. Create pillar content pages for top 3 keyword clusters (TIM, electrochemical, die attach)
2. Invest in REACH compliance content — minimal competition, high commercial intent
3. Build case study content featuring client success stories (with permission from Apple, Intel, etc.)
4. Target SpecialChem content gaps for GEO — their content is broad; CAPLINQ's is deeper

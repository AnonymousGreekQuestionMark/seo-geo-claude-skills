## Full Findings

### Blog Content Competitors

| Competitor | Strength | CAPLINQ Advantage |
|------------|---------|------------------|
| Henkel Technical Blog | Deep product knowledge; manufacturer perspective | CAPLINQ: Neutral multi-supplier view |
| 3M/Nitto Technical Notes | High authority; PDF-heavy | CAPLINQ: Web-native, indexable HTML content |
| ResearchGate/Academia.edu | Academic depth; citation credibility | CAPLINQ: Practical application focus; readable |
| SpecialChem Blog | SEO-optimized; broad coverage | CAPLINQ: Deeper technical specificity |
| ElectronicsWeekly/EDN | High domain authority | CAPLINQ: More application-specific depth |

### Key Insight: CAPLINQ Blog's Unique Positioning
The PFAS-free GDL coating post is a standout example of CAPLINQ's content differentiation:
- **Original R&D data** (water contact angle 154.4° vs competitor 153.8°; HFR 1.8 kΩ)
- **Practical application guidance** (not just theory)
- **Named technical author** (Rose Anne Acedera, Technical Applications Engineer)
- **Proprietary findings** not available elsewhere

This is the type of content that earns links from academic papers and industry publications. CAPLINQ should produce more posts in this format.

### Content Format Gap vs. Competitors
- Manufacturer blogs: heavy use of infographics, comparison tables → CAPLINQ needs more visual content
- SpecialChem: strong SEO meta tags, internal linking to product database → CAPLINQ needs better blog-to-product linking
- High-authority publications: guest-contributed expert roundups → CAPLINQ could leverage its client relationships

### Recommended Competitive Actions
1. Pitch the PFAS-free GDL research to Electronics Weekly or Chemical Engineering for coverage
2. Create visual comparison infographics for die attach types and TIM types (shareable, link-worthy)
3. Build "expert commentary" posts featuring CAPLINQ engineers commenting on industry announcements
